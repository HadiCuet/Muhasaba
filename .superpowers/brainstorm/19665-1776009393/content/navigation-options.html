A. Bottom Tab Bar

Persistent tabs at the bottom. One tap to switch. The standard pattern for iOS (UITabBarController) and most Android apps.

Pros

  • Familiar — iOS muscle memory
  • All sections always one tap away
  • Shows where you are at a glance
  • FAB sits naturally above the bar

Cons

  • Takes ~56px of vertical space permanently
  • 4 tabs is the sweet spot (we have exactly 4)

B. Keep Current (AppBar Icons)

History, Stats, Settings stay as small icons in the top app bar. Today is the hub; other screens are push-to-navigate.

Pros

  • Maximum vertical space for amal list
  • Simple — no refactoring
  • Works fine for a personal app

Cons

  • Icons are small and easy to miss
  • No sense of "where am I"
  • Must hit back to return to Today
  • Feels like an afterthought

C. Navigation Drawer

Hamburger menu slides out from the left. Lists all destinations with labels and icons. Common on Android/Material apps.

Pros

  • Full vertical space when closed
  • Room for branding/profile at top
  • Scales well if you add more screens later

Cons

  • Hidden — out of sight, out of mind
  • Two taps to switch (open + tap)
  • Feels dated for a 4-tab app
  • Unfamiliar to iOS users

⭐ Recommendation: Bottom Tab Bar (A)

With exactly 4 destinations, a bottom tab bar is the textbook Material 3 / iOS pattern. It matches your iOS background (UITabBarController), keeps every section one tap away, and gives spatial orientation — the user always knows where they are. The FAB for "New amal" floats above the bar on the Today tab only.
